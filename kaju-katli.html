Kaju Katli

A smooth and melt-in-the-mouth Indian sweet made with cashews, perfect for festive occasions!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cashews (raw, unsalted)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 teaspoon rose water (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon ghee (for greasing)
  • Silver vark (optional, for decoration)

Instructions:

Prepare the Cashew Powder:
  1. Grind the cashews into a fine powder using a blender or food processor. Avoid over-blending to prevent the release of oils.
Make the Sugar Syrup:
  1. In a non-stick pan, combine sugar and water and cook on medium heat until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Simmer until the syrup reaches a one-string consistency.
Mix the Dough:
  1. Lower the heat and gradually add the cashew powder to the syrup, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  2. Add rose water if using, and continue stirring until the mixture thickens and forms a soft dough.
Shape the Kaju Katli:
  1. Grease a flat surface or a large plate with ghee.
  2. Transfer the dough onto the surface and knead it gently while still warm.
  3. Roll the dough into a thin sheet using a greased rolling pin.
  4. Apply silver vark for decoration if desired and let it cool completely.
  5. Cut into diamond shapes using a sharp knife.
Serve:
  • Store in an airtight container or serve immediately as a delightful dessert.
Enjoy your delicious Kaju Katli!
