Masala-Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian breakfast dish that consists of a crispy, thin, and savory crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, filled with a flavorful potato masala (spiced potato filling). It's often served with sambar (a lentil-based vegetable stew) and coconut chutney.

Ingredients:-

For the batter:
  • 1 cup idli rice (short-grain rice)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpea)
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi dana)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • Water for soaking and grinding
For the potato Masala:
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and peeled
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Instructions:

Making the Batter:
  1. Soak:Rinse the rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds separately in water for at least 4 hours.
  2. Grind:Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them into a smooth batter using a wet grinder or a blender. The batter should be slightly thick and have a smooth, pouring consistency.
  3. Ferment:Transfer the batter to a large bowl, cover it with a damp cloth, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours, or overnight. The batter should become fluffy and bubbly.
Making the potato masala:
  1. Saute:Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds, and let them splutter. Add the chopped onion, green chili, and ginger, and saut√© until the onion turns golden brown.
  2. Spice it up:Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Stir well.
  3. Mash:Add the boiled and mashed potatoes to the pan and mix well. Cook for a few more minutes until the flavors are well combined.
  4. Garnish:Add chopped coriander leaves and remove from heat.
Cooking the Masala-Dosa:
  1. Heat the griddle:Heat a non-stick tawa or griddle over medium heat.
  2. Pour the Batter:Pour a ladleful of batter onto the hot griddle and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin, round crepe.
  3. Cook:Cook the dosa until it is golden brown and crispy on both sides.
  4. Add the fillingPlace a spoonful of the potato masala in the center of the dosa and fold it in half.
  5. Serve:Serve the hot masala dosa with coconut chutney and sambar.
Tips:
  • For a crispier dosa, add a little oil to the batter
  • If the batter is too thick, add a little water.
  • If the batter is too thin, add a little more rice flour
  • Experiment with different fillings like vegetable stuffing or paneer masala.
Enjoy Your homemade Masala-Dosa !!
