Gulab Jamun

A classic Indian dessert made with soft, deep-fried milk dumplings soaked in sugar syrup.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup milk powder
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tbsp ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2-3 tbsp milk
  • Oil or ghee for frying
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 4-5 cardamom pods, crushed
  • Few drops of rose water
  • Chopped pistachios for garnish (optional)

Instructions

Prepare the dough:
  1. In a mixing bowl, combine milk powder, all-purpose flour, and baking soda.
  2. Add ghee and mix until the mixture resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add milk, one tablespoon at a time, and knead into a soft dough. Cover and set aside.
Make the sugar syrup:
  1. In a pan, combine sugar and water. Heat until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Add crushed cardamom pods and simmer the syrup for 5 minutes.
  3. Remove from heat and stir in rose water. Keep the syrup warm.
Shape and fry the Gulab Jamun:
  1. Divide the dough into small, equal portions and roll into smooth balls without cracks.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan over medium-low heat.
  3. Gently drop the balls into the hot oil and fry until golden brown, stirring occasionally.
  4. Remove and drain on a paper towel.
Soak in syrup:
  1. Immerse the fried balls in the warm sugar syrup.
  2. Let them soak for at least 2 hours to absorb the syrup fully.
Serve:
  • Serve warm or chilled, garnished with chopped pistachios.
  • Enjoy the delicious, soft Gulab Jamuns with your loved ones!
