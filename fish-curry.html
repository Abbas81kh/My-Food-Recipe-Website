Fish Curry

Fish curry is a flavorful dish made with tender fish cooked in a rich, spiced gravy. The curry base typically includes ingredients like onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, and a variety of spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, and chili powder. The fish is simmered in the curry until cooked through, and the dish is often finished with a touch of coconut milk or tamarind for a tangy richness. It is commonly served with rice or flatbreads.

Ingredients:-

For the fish:
  • 1 pound firm white fish (cod, halibut, tilapia), cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
For the curry
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the fish:In a bowl, combine the fish pieces with salt, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Mix well to coat. Set aside
  2. Make the curry:Heat the oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened and golden brown, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and ginger, and cook for 30 seconds more.
  4. Stir in the cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, and red chili powder. Cook for 30 seconds, until fragrant.
  5. Add the tomatoes and cook until they break down and form a thick sauce, about 5 minutes
  6. Stir in the coconut milk and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 10 minutes.
  7. Add the marinated fish pieces to the curry, making sure they are submerged in the sauce. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through.
  8. Season with salt to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve immediately.
Tips:
  • You can use any firm white fish for this recipe.
  • If you don't have coconut milk, you can use heavy cream or evaporated milk.
  • Adjust the amount of red chili powder to your desired level of spiciness
  • This curry is best served with steamed rice or naan.
Enjoy your homemade Fish Curry!!
