Motichoor Ladoo

A classic Indian sweet made with tiny gram flour pearls, sugar syrup, and ghee, perfect for any celebration!

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups besan (gram flour)
  • 1/2 cup water (for batter)
  • 2 cups sugar
  • 1 cup water (for sugar syrup)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Orange food color (optional)
  • 1/4 cup ghee (for frying)
  • Chopped pistachios or almonds for garnishing

Instructions:

Prepare the Batter:
  1. In a bowl, mix besan, water, and a pinch of orange food color to form a smooth batter without lumps.
Make the Boondis:
  1. Heat ghee in a deep pan over medium heat.
  2. Use a perforated ladle to pour the batter into the hot ghee, forming small droplets (boondis).
  3. Fry the boondis until lightly golden, then drain on a paper towel. Repeat with remaining batter.
Prepare the Sugar Syrup:
  1. In a pan, combine sugar and water and heat until the sugar dissolves completely.
  2. Simmer until the syrup reaches a one-string consistency.
  3. Add cardamom powder and a few drops of food color to the syrup.
Combine and Shape:
  1. Mix the boondis into the sugar syrup and cook for 2-3 minutes, ensuring they absorb the syrup.
  2. Allow the mixture to cool slightly, then grease your palms with ghee.
  3. Shape the mixture into round ladoos while it is still warm.
Garnish and Serve:
  • Decorate each ladoo with chopped pistachios or almonds.
  • Store in an airtight container or serve fresh during festivities.
Enjoy your delightful Motichoor Ladoos!
