Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken or Murgha Makhani is a popular North Indian dish made with tender chicken cooked in rice, Creamy tomato-based gravy. The chicken is marinated with yogurt and spices grilled, and then simmered in sauce flavored with butter, cream and garam masala. its best Enjoyed with naan or rice.

Ingredients:

For the marinade:
  • 1 kg boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powde
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • Salt to taste

    • For the gravy:
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp turmeric powde
  • 1 cup tomato puree
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken:In a bowl, combine all the marinade ingredients. Add the chicken pieces and marinate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight.
  2. Cook the chicken:Heat a pan or grill and cook the marinated chicken pieces until they are cooked through and slightly charred.
  3. Prepare the gravy:Heat 2 tablespoons of butter in a pot. Add the onion, garlic, and ginger and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add Spices:Add cumin seeds, coriander powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and turmeric powder. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  5. Add tomato puree:Pour in the tomato puree and cook until the oil separates
  6. Combine with chicken:Add the cooked chicken pieces to the gravy, along with the marinade.
  7. Simmer and thcken:Add water to adjust the consistency of the gravy. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 10-15 minutes, or until the sauce thickens.
  8. Finish with cream and butter:Stir in heavy cream and 2 tablespoons of butter. Add kasuri methi and salt to taste.
  9. Serve:Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice
Tips:
  • For a richer flavor, you can use ghee instead of butter.
  • To make the gravy smoother, you can blend it with a hand blender.
  • Adjust the spice level according to your taste.
  • For a smoky flavor, you can char the chicken pieces over an open flame
Enjoy your homemade Butter Chicken!!
