Rasmalai

A luscious Indian dessert made of soft cheese dumplings soaked in sweetened flavored milk!

Ingredients:

  • 1 liter full-fat milk (for rasmalai balls)
  • 1 liter full-fat milk (for the rabri)
  • 2-3 tbsp lemon juice or vinegar
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3-4 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 cup chopped pistachios and almonds
  • A few saffron strands (optional)

Instructions:

Prepare the Rasmalai Balls:
  1. Boil 1 liter of milk and add lemon juice or vinegar to curdle it.
  2. Strain the curdled milk using a muslin cloth to separate the chenna (paneer).
  3. Rinse the chenna under cold water to remove the sourness and squeeze out excess water.
  4. Knead the chenna for 8-10 minutes until smooth and soft.
  5. Divide the chenna into small equal portions and roll into smooth balls, flattening slightly.
Cook the Rasmalai Balls:
  1. In a large pan, boil 3-4 cups of water with 1 cup of sugar to make sugar syrup.
  2. Drop the chenna balls into the boiling sugar syrup and cook for 10-12 minutes on medium heat.
  3. Turn off the heat and let the balls cool in the syrup.
Prepare the Rabri:
  1. Boil 1 liter of milk in a heavy-bottomed pan and simmer on low heat until it reduces by half.
  2. Add 1/4 cup of sugar, cardamom powder, saffron strands, and chopped nuts. Mix well.
Assemble the Rasmalai:
  • Gently squeeze the syrup out of the cooked rasmalai balls and add them to the rabri.
  • Let the rasmalai soak in the rabri for 1-2 hours in the refrigerator.
  • Garnish with more nuts before serving.
Enjoy your delicious homemade Rasmalai!
