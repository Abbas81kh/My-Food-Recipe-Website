idli

Idli is a popular South Indian breakfast food known for its soft, fluffy texture and mild flavor. It's made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram), which is steamed in special molds to create small, cylindrical cakes. Idli is often served with sambar, a lentil-based vegetable stew, and coconut chutney, a tangy and spicy condiment. It's a healthy and easily digestible option, making it a popular choice for people of all ages.

Ingredients:-

For the idli:
  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 3 cups parboiled rice
  • 1 tablespoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • water

Instructions:

  1. Soak and grind: Rinse the urad dal and rice separately until the water runs clear.
  2. Add the fenugreek seeds to the urad dal.
  3. Soak the urad dal and rice in separate bowls of water for at least 4-6 hours.
  4. Drain the water and grind the urad dal into a smooth paste. Add a little water if needed.
  5. Grind the rice into a coarse batter.
  6. Combine the urad dal and rice batters in a large bowl. Add salt to taste and mix well.
Formet the Batter:-
  1. Cover the batter and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-10 hours, or until it becomes frothy and doubles in volume.
Steam the idli:
  1. Grease the idli molds with oil.
  2. Pour the batter into the molds, filling them about three-quarters full.
  3. Place the idli molds in the steamer and steam for 10-12 minutes, or the idlis are cooked through.
  4. Remove the idlis from the steamer and let them cool slightly before serving.
Serve: Serve hot with sambhar and coconut chutney.
    Tips:
  • For softer idlis, grind the urad dal to a finer consistency.
  • Don't overfill the idli molds, as the batter will expand during steaming.
  • If the batter is too thick, add a little water to thin it out.
  • Experiment with different types of rice and urad dal to find your favorite combination. Enjoy your homemade idlis!
