Haleem

A rich and flavorful stew made with wheat, lentils, and meat, slow-cooked to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup wheat (broken)
  • 1/2 cup split Bengal gram (chana dal)
  • 1/2 cup split pigeon peas (toor dal)
  • 1/2 cup split green gram (moong dal)
  • 500g mutton (with bones)
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • 3 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • 4-5 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander and fried onions for garnish
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Instructions:

Prepare the Grains and Lentils:

  1. Wash the wheat and lentils thoroughly. Soak them for 1 hour.
  2. Cook the soaked wheat and lentils in a pressure cooker with water and a pinch of salt until soft.
  3. Blend the cooked mixture slightly for a creamy texture. Set aside.

Cook the Mutton:

  1. Heat ghee or oil in a large pot. Add the sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  2. Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears.
  3. Add the mutton pieces, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, coriander powder, and cumin powder. Mix well.
  4. Add water, cover, and cook until the mutton is tender.

Combine and Cook Haleem:

  1. Add the cooked wheat and lentil mixture to the pot with mutton. Mix thoroughly.
  2. Cook on low heat for 30-40 minutes, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
  3. Adjust the consistency with water as needed. Add salt to taste.

Garnish and Serve:

  • Garnish with fresh coriander and fried onions.
  • Serve hot with lemon wedges and naan or paratha.
Enjoy your hearty Haleem!
