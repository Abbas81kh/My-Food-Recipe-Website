Aloo Gobi

A traditional and flavorful Indian dish made with potatoes and cauliflower, perfect for any meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cauliflower florets (cleaned and chopped)
  • 2 medium-sized potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil (vegetable or mustard oil works best)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed or finely chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 cup green peas (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Wash the cauliflower and potatoes thoroughly. Cut them into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Heat oil: In a large pan, heat the oil and add cumin seeds. Let them sizzle for a few seconds.
  3. Sauté onions: Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  4. Cook the tomatoes: Add the tomato puree and cook for about 5 minutes until the oil starts to separate.
  5. Add spices: Mix in turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Stir well.
  6. Add vegetables: Add the potatoes and cauliflower to the pan. Stir to coat them evenly with the spices.
  7. Add green peas: If using, mix in the green peas at this stage for extra texture and flavor.
  8. Cover and cook: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let the vegetables cook for about 20 minutes. Stir occasionally.
  9. Add garam masala: Sprinkle garam masala over the cooked vegetables and mix gently.
  10. Garnish: Remove from heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  11. Serve: Serve hot with roti, naan, or steamed rice.
    Tips for Perfect Aloo Gobi:
  • Use fresh and firm cauliflower and potatoes for the best texture.
  • Roast the cauliflower in a little oil before adding to the pan for extra flavor.
  • To make it healthier, use less oil and steam the vegetables instead of frying.
  • Adding a dash of lemon juice before serving enhances the flavors.
  • Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days in the refrigerator.
Enjoy your homemade Aloo Gobi!!
